Getting Started with Extract Brewing

In this lesson, we'll introduce the basic concepts of malt extract brewing, as well as go over the equipment you'll need to get started.
Estimated Lesson Time: 15 min.

Mark As Completed


What is malt extract brewing?
Brewing with malt extract is by far the easiest way to get started making beer – in fact the majority of home brewers started this way. Malt extract is the product of mashing barley that has been partially germinated, dried out, and roasted – then steeped to extract the natural sugars. The liquid from the steep is then reduced until it consists of thick syrup, which is the primary ingredient we use to make the sweet liquid called wort.
The basic process consists of just boiling the malt, water, and hops together. Hops are added to the wort (the term for the sweet liquid before it becomes beer) at various stages of the boil to add bitterness, flavor and aroma. Finally, the last step will be to pitch your yeast into the cooled wort, and let the magic happen! Pretty straight forward right?

If you are interested in learning how malt extract is made, check out this article from the Home Brewers Association.

What equipment do I need?
Aside from the pot you’ll need to boil the wort, you’re starter kit includes everything you need to brew your first batch of beer: a 2 gallon fermentation bucket, 1 airlock, a thermometer, hydrometer, test tube, auto siphon, sanitizer, priming sugar, 2 64oz plastic growlers, and your first recipe kit. As your brewing knowledge grows, most likely so will your brewing setup. Since we are just starting out with extract brewing; only minimal equipment is needed.
